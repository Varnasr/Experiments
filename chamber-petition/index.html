Subject: Request for Reconsideration of the Decision to Allot Work-Stations in Lieu of Chambers to Advocates and Advocates-on-Record

Respected Hon'ble Chief Justice,

We, the undersigned Advocates practising before this Hon'ble Court respectfully submit this representation seeking reconsideration of the decision by the competent authority to construct and allot only work-stations, rather than individual chambers, to members of the Bar.

I. The Practical Hardship

Members of the Bar, particularly younger practitioners, face acute difficulty in securing office space in Delhi proximate to the Supreme Court. The waiting period for chamber allotment currently extends to 15–25 years after their enrolment as advocates. This protracted delay imposes significant professional and financial constraints on advocates-on-record who must nevertheless comply with Order IV, Rule 5, (iii) of the Supreme Court Rules, 1966, which mandates that Advocates-on-Record maintain an office within 16 kilometres of the Court.

II. The Inadequacy of Work-Stations

A work-station, by its very nature, cannot substitute for a chamber. Advocates require private, secure spaces to consult with clients, preserve confidentiality, store case records, and conduct preparatory work with requisite dignity. After decades of practice, the allotment of a mere work-station would neither address these professional necessities nor serve any meaningful purpose.

III. A Constructive Proposal

It is respectfully submitted that the land available near the I.P. Extension Metro Station for Supreme Court lawyers Chamber, may be utilised more effectively if individual chambers of approximately 90 square feet are constructed on a single-allotment basis, the site could accommodate nearly 1,000 advocates. Such an allocation would substantially alleviate the prevailing shortage and materially improve the working conditions of younger practitioners.

IV. Prayer

In light of the above, we humbly pray that Your Lordship may reconsider the present decision and explore the feasibility of constructing individual chambers in place of work-stations.

We remain grateful for Your Lordship's kind consideration of this representation.

Respectfully submitted,
[Names and Signatures of Undersigned Advocates-on-Record]
